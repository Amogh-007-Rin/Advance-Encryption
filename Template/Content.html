What is Advanced Encryption?

Advanced encryption is a method of securing digital data by transforming it into an unreadable format using complex algorithms. This ensures that only authorized parties with the correct decryption key can access the original information. It's widely used on websites through protocols like HTTPS, which relies on technologies such as TLS (Transport Layer Security) and encryption algorithms like AES and RSA. These systems protect sensitive data—like login details, payment info, and personal messages—while it's being sent over the internet, shielding it from hackers, surveillance, or tampering.
